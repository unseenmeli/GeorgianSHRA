The Georgian Association of Reproductive and Sexual Health

Established on June 24, 2022 | Tbilisi, Georgia

Mission

The mission of the Association is to advance scientific research, education, and clinical practice in the field of reproductive and sexual health, and to improve the quality and accessibility of reproductive and sexual health services across Georgia through professional collaboration, education and evidence-based practice.

Objectives

• Promotion of the exchange of scientific knowledge and clinical expertise among professionals in reproductive and sexual health through structured professional communication, cooperation, and educational programs.

• Organization and support of scientific conferences, congresses, training courses, seminars, and workshops at national and international levels for physicians, medical students, residents, nurses, and other healthcare professionals.

• Advancement of research initiatives, collaborative projects, and the exchange of professional experience among members of the Association.

• Strengthening of national and international professional networks through enhanced cooperation and communication among experts in reproductive and sexual health worldwide.

• Implementation of public education and awareness initiatives aimed at increasing understanding of contemporary approaches, services, and preventive strategies in reproductive and sexual health.

• Collaboration with national and international organizations, academic institutions, and experts through joint projects and initiatives contributing to the global advancement of reproductive and sexual health.

• Support for the publication and dissemination of scientific books, journals, articles, guidelines, and other educational or multimedia materials relevant to the objectives of the Association.

• Participation in and cooperation with other entities, associations, and organizations pursuing similar or related objectives.

• Development, adaptation, and dissemination of theoretical, methodological, and educational materials based on modern international and domestic evidence-based medical practices, including the sourcing, translation, adaptation, and publication of relevant content.
